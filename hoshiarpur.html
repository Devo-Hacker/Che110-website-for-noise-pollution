Hoshiarpur

Hoshiarpur, a city in Punjab, India, faces noise pollution due to various factors. Here are some main reasons contributing to noise pollution in Hoshiarpur:
1. Vehicular Traffic: The increasing number of vehicles on the road, including trucks, buses, cars, and two-wheelers, contributes significantly to noise pollution.

2. Construction and Infrastructure Development: Building activities, road construction, and urban development projects generate substantial noise.

3. Industrial Activities: Hoshiarpur has various industries, such as textile mills, steel fabrication units, and food processing plants, which produce noise.

4. Agricultural Activities: Farm machinery, such as tractors and harvesters, contribute to noise pollution.

5. Festivals and Celebrations: Loudspeakers and firecrackers during festivals and celebrations add to the noise.

6. Market and Commercial Areas: Busy markets, shopping centers, and commercial areas with loudspeakers, announcements, and vehicular movement generate noise.

7. Rail and Air Traffic: Proximity to railway lines and airports contributes to noise pollution.

8. Loud Music and Public Address Systems: Loud music from public events, weddings, and public address systems contribute to noise pollution.

9. Generators and Pumps: Diesel generators and agricultural pumps create noise, especially during power outages.

10. Lack of Enforcement of Noise Regulations: Inadequate enforcement of noise pollution laws and regulations exacerbates the issue.

Rural Region

Rural Areas Average Decibels: Rural areas generally have lower noise levels, often between 40 to 60 dB, depending on the time of day and activities.

Common Sound Sources: Nature sounds, agricultural machinery, and occasional vehicle noise.

Urban Regions

Average Decibels: Urban environments tend to have higher noise levels, often ranging from 60 to 85 dB during the day. Peak levels can sometimes exceed 90 dB in busy areas.

Common Sound Sources: Traffic, construction, street vendors, and public transportation.
