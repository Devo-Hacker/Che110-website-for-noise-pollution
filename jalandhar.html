Jalandhar

Jalandhar, a city in Punjab, India, faces noise pollution due to various factors. Here are some main reasons contributing to noise pollution in Jalandhar:
Industrial and Vehicular Sources:

1. Vehicular traffic: Increasing number of vehicles, trucks, buses, and two-wheelers.
2. Industrial activities: Textile mills, steel fabrication units, and manufacturing plants.
3. Railway and air traffic: Proximity to railway lines and Adampur Airport.

Commercial and Social Sources:
1. Market and commercial areas: Busy markets, shopping centers, and loudspeakers.
2. Festivals and celebrations: Loudspeakers, firecrackers, and processions.
3. Public address systems: Loud music from public events, weddings, and announcements.
4. Construction activities: Building construction, road repair, and urban development.

Agricultural and Domestic Sources:
1. Farm machinery: Tractors, harvesters, and other agricultural equipment.\
2. Generators and pumps: Diesel generators and agricultural pumps.
3. Domestic noise: Loud music, televisions, and household appliances. br

Urban Region

Urban Areas in Jalandhar

Sources of Noise Pollution: Major roads, commercial areas, markets, factories, and construction sites.
Typical Noise Levels: In urban areas, the noise levels often range from 70 to 90 decibels (dB). Near busy intersections or construction zones, it can go even higher, sometimes surpassing 100 dB during peak hours.
Impacts: Prolonged exposure to these levels can cause health issues such as stress, hearing impairment, and sleep disturbances.

Rural Regions

Rural Areas around Jalandhar

Sources of Noise Pollution:
Farming machinery (tractors, threshers), occasional road traffic, and village gatherings or events.
Typical Noise Levels: Rural areas generally have lower noise levels, averaging around 40 to 60 dB. At peak times, such as during harvest or festivals, it might briefly rise to 70 dB but usually does not sustain at high levels.
Impacts: Noise pollution in rural areas is generally less intense, with fewer adverse health impacts.
